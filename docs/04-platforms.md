## 4.2 Honest Breakdown

### ✅ Strongly Recommended

- **KodeKloud** — The best money you'll spend for DevOps. Browser-based labs (no setup), CKA/CKAD prep, Docker, K8s, Terraform, Jenkins. Learning DevOps by *doing*, not watching. 💼🚀
- **Udemy** — Unbeatable value when courses are on sale. The standout instructors: **Stephane Maarek** (AWS/Kafka), **Chad Darby** (Spring), **Tim Buchalka** (Java), **Mumshad Mannambeth** (DevOps). ⚠️ Quality varies wildly — buy by instructor, not topic.
- **Baeldung** — The reference for Spring/Java in writing. Concise, accurate, code-first. Free tier covers most needs.

### ⚖️ Situational

- **Educative** — Excellent for **system design** (Grokking series) and text learners who hate video. Subscription only.
- **Spring Academy** — Authoritative, but the official docs + guides are often enough.
- **Pluralsight** — Great structured paths for enterprise teams; the Skill IQ assessments help. Worth it if your employer pays.
- **Coursera** — Best for credentials/degrees and CS fundamentals. You can **audit most courses free**. Slower-paced.
- **LinkedIn Learning** — Often free via public libraries; good for breadth and soft skills, shallow on deep backend.
- **GeeksforGeeks** — Fantastic as a quick-lookup and interview-question reference; ⚠️ inconsistent depth/accuracy — verify against docs.

### ❌ Not Recommended (for this goal)

- **Udacity** — Quality is decent but the price rarely justifies it versus Udemy + KodeKloud.
- **Codecademy** — Built for beginners; you're past it. Outgrow it quickly.

## 4.3 Recommended Stack (what to actually buy)

> The minimalist, high-ROI combo:

| Purpose | Pick | Why |
|---------|------|-----|
| Java + Spring depth | **Baeldung (free)** + 1–2 Udemy courses | Best signal-to-noise |
| DevOps hands-on | **KodeKloud** subscription | Labs you can't get elsewhere |
| AWS | **Udemy (Maarek SAA-C03)** + free AWS Skill Builder | Cert-aligned, practical |
| System Design | **Educative (Grokking)** or **ByteByteGo** + DDIA book | Interview-critical |
| Daily reference | **Official docs + YouTube (TechWorld with Nana, Hussein Nasser)** | Free, current |

**Total realistic spend:** ~$30–60/month during active learning, or far less if you time Udemy sales and use free tiers. 💼

> ⚠️ **Common Mistake:** Buying 15 courses and finishing none ("course hoarding"). Own **one active course per topic** at a time and complete it before buying the next.
